Behind the Design

About This Website Prototype

This prototype reimagines the Birmingham Public Library website with a modern, welcoming, and community-focused experience. The goal was to improve usability while making library services easier to discover through clear navigation, engaging visuals, and interactive features. The design balances functionality, accessibility, and community engagement while demonstrating how the library's online presence can better support patrons.

Design & Development Process

My goal was to create a clean, inviting website that immediately connects visitors with the library's most frequently used services while presenting Birmingham Public Library as an innovative and community-centered organization. The project began by identifying the library services patrons use most frequently and organizing them into a clear homepage structure. The interface was developed using an iterative process, beginning with wireframe concepts and gradually adding responsive layouts, interactive features, and live resource integrations. Throughout development, functionality and usability were tested continuously to ensure navigation remained intuitive while maintaining a clean, modern appearance.

User Experience Considerations

User experience was a primary focus throughout the project. Frequently accessed services such as catalog searching, WiFi printing, meeting rooms, and digital resources were placed prominently to reduce the number of clicks required to reach them. Responsive layouts, semantic HTML, descriptive labels, accessible color contrast, and keyboard-friendly navigation were incorporated to improve usability for a wide range of patrons and devices.

Design & Technical Decisions

Key Design & Technical Considerations

The prototype combines modern visual design with practical functionality. Glassmorphism effects, contemporary typography, and consistent color palettes were chosen to create an inviting and professional appearance while maintaining readability. Interactive elements, real external links, and a live catalog search were incorporated to demonstrate how the website could function as more than a visual mockup. The overall design emphasizes discoverability, accessibility, and community engagement.

Primary Design Goals

Modern Appearance

Create a visually engaging interface using glassmorphism, modern typography, and clean layouts.

Easy Navigation

Allow patrons to reach important services quickly with intuitive navigation and clear visual organization.

Accessibility

Support readable typography, semantic HTML, keyboard accessibility, and responsive layouts for all users.

Community Focus

Highlight library programs, outreach initiatives, digital resources, and services that strengthen community engagement.

03

Why These Features?

WiFi Printing

Positioned prominently because it is one of the library's most frequently used services. Visitors should be able to locate printing immediately without searching through multiple pages.

Branch Programs

Encourages community engagement by showcasing events taking place throughout the Birmingham Public Library system.

Digital Library

Provides quick access to eBooks, audiobooks, streaming media, online learning resources, and research databases available 24/7.

Library of Things

Highlights one of Birmingham Public Library's unique collections while increasing awareness of resources beyond traditional books.

Database of the Month

Promotes valuable research resources that many patrons may not realize are available through the library.
